what it is

peer is a real agent — not a wrapper around a chat box. It plans, picks tools, runs them, reads results, and decides what's next. The 17 tools are research tools: map a field, find a gap, deep-read a paper, draft related work, find collaborators. Local-first. BYOK. Apache 2.0.

It's not a coding agent. It's not a chatbot. It's not another paper-search wrapper. It's an agent for the research journey — from "I have no idea what to work on" to your defense. It calibrates to your role (PhD student, postdoc, faculty, industry, independent) from a one-line profile.

17 research tools

map, gap, brainstorm, read, ask, compare, cite, relwork, outline, collab, daily, next, journal, history, graph, doctor, onboard.

compounding library

4-layer knowledge graph in local SQLite. Notes in plain Markdown (Obsidian-compatible). Your library grows for years.

sandboxed agent

No shell tool. File operations path-jailed to your peer home. The agent cannot touch anything outside ~/.peer/.

$0.05 per field

A 2,000-word PhD-quality field overview + 40-paper tiered reading list. 38 seconds. You pay your provider directly.

built on pi-agent-core

A minimal extensible agent runtime. The same substrate as coding agents — peer just has research-shaped tools.

local · BYOK · Apache 2.0

Your keys, your spend, your machine, your library. Free forever. Fork it, ship it, build on it.

install

one line. node 22+ required.

$ curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/KeWang0622/peer/main/scripts/install/install.sh | sh

Set your keys (BYOK):
export ANTHROPIC_API_KEY=sk-ant-... · required, the agent's brain
export OPENAI_API_KEY=sk-... · required for peer ask and peer map embeddings
export SEMANTIC_SCHOLAR_API_KEY=... · optional, raises rate limits

Then: peer doctor → peer onboard → you're on the journey.
